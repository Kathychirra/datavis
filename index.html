The Disappearing Lakes of Bangalore

Water, a resource so close to us, not only crucial to our day to day activities but also for our sustenance as a community. Our lives cannot go one without water. But, how is it so that a resource so close to us is exploited without any caution? Today, many cities in India are facing an acute water crisis and the so called ‘Garden City’ of Bengaluru is so exception.

In the 16th century, Kempegowda built tanks and irrigation wells as well. Traditionally in interlinked through a chain or cascade system, this ensured water was not wasted. These lakes were a part of the community, they irrigated fields, took care of the environment and were sources of livelihoods.

Over time, most of these lakes have disappeared due to encroachment, blockage of connecting channels and so on. With just about 153 lakes left today of 980 in 1950-1960. Most of them are in really bad shape with sewage and waste flowing into them, destroying the environment, ecosystem and serving no purpose to us as well. According to a report by IISC,Bengaluru is said to run out of ground water by the end of 2020 and out of portable drinking water by 2030, creating an alarming situation for us to act upon
